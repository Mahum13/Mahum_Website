MAHUM HASHMI
Hi! My name is Mahum Hashmi and I am currently a postgraduate student at Goldsmiths, University of London doing an MA in Computer Games, Art and Design. I hold a Bachelor of Science degree from King's College London in Computer Science.

This website is a portfolio of work I have done in game development, 3D modelling and animation, along with various software engineering projects. There is also a section showcasing projects I have done in music production and music videos, as a cover artist.
GAME DEVELOPMENT

"The Shining Experience" - Virtual Reality

This project was inspired by Stanley Kubrick's 1980 film "The Shining" which is based on the novel by Stephen King. This is a Virtual Reality experience where the player is immersed into the Overlook Hotel and can explore the various rooms.

Lighting and sound were the focus of this project, to use them as a means to create an immersive horror experience. Triggering sound and lighting effects along with animations to help guide the player's exploration was a particular focus as well.

Software: Unity (VR Toolkit; C#); Demo on the Oculus Rift headset

"Shoot the Song"

This is a first-person view musical game where the player has to hit the musical targets in the correct order to open the door.
When the level loads, an audio track is played - in this video it is "I'll Be There For You" by the Rembrandts. The player must then explore the environment to find 4 targets which each hold a different snippet of the original audio. These must be hit in the correct order in order to progress.
The player can then switch weapons in order to complete the same challenge, except this time the snippets are of a specific instrument in the original audio. For example in this video, it is the rhythm guitar component of the arrangement. In the middle of the environment, there is a blow horn which will be play what the second track should sound like as a hint.
Once both tracks are correctly sound, the door opens completely.

Software: Unreal Engine 4 (Blueprints)

"Escape from Neverland" - Parser Game

Escape from Neverland is a parser game based on the book "Peter Pan" by J.M Barrie (also the popular 1953 animated Disney movie). Set in the fictional world of Neverland, the player plays as Wendy with the objective of flying home. However, to do so, you must get Tinkerbell's magical fairy dust. This takes the player on adventures all over the island, fighting pirates, mermaids and meeting friends on the way.
As you move around the environment, you can pick up different items and add them to your satchel, however there is a limit to how much you can carry. You must fight pirates to protect yourself and your friends, and fight mermaids to fulfil Tinkerbell's demands.
There is also a 'transportation' space, where once you enter you are transported to a random setting on the island, and face different challenges.

Language: Java

"Finding Memory"

A first person view game, Finding Memory is essentially a 'finding things' game. You are shown three items in the beginning of the level, which are displayed for a short amount of time.
Once you enter the environment, which in this demo is a simple living room and kitchen area, you must find the items that were shown in the beginning.

Software: Unity (C#).

3D MODELLING

These models were my first attempt at 3D modelling. The assets are inspired by Alice in Wonderland, with the White Rabbit's pocket-watch and Alice's 'drink me' bottle.

Software: Maya

The assets above are made to show different traditional Pakistani objects. All were created to have different uses in a game environment.
The first asset are a pair of jhoomkas which are traditional Pakistani earrings worn by Pakistani women not only on special occasions, but are also incorporated in their everyday lives. In a game, this asset is intended to be used as a reward or 'treasure' to be found.
The second asset is a mutka which is a clay pot. The texture on this asset is traditional Pakistani Truck Art, done by the painter Haider Ali. This style of painting is known as truck art because it is mostly found on big transport trucks all over the country and is a big part of Pakistani art and culture. This asset would be used as a weapon or tool to break through walls or other hard surfaces in a game.
The third asset is a janamaz which is an Islamic prayer rug. When Muslims pray on this mat, no matter where they are in the world, they must point the rug in the direction of the Holy Ka'aba (House of God) located in Makkah, Saudi Arabia. Using this idea of direction, in a game environment this asset would be used as a navigation tool to guide the player.
The fourth asset is a dhol which is a large barrel shaped drum. A very popular instrument in Pakistani music, a dhol can be played with hands or sticks. In a game, a player would play the dhol to open a door.

Software: Maya

ANIMATION

Player Swing Animation

The 'swing' asset in this animation is inspired by traditional Pakistani swings. This asset was designed to be used as a 'launcher' in a game, launching the player onto a higher platform.
The player in this animation was rigged using the Advanced Skeleton Tools plugin installed from the Animation Studios' website: https://www.animationstudios.com.au/advanced-skeleton-download.
The animation sequence was made on Maya and imported into Unity, where player inputs were used to trigger the animation.

Software: Maya, Unity(C#)

Player Dhol Animation

The barrel shaped drum in this animation is a Pakistani instrument known as a dhol, which the player plays on in order to open a door. The dhol was modelled in Maya, whereas the door was modelled in Unity.
The character animation was made in Maya and imported into Unity to be triggered by player inputs.

Software: Maya, Unity(C#)

Tin Man Animation

This animation sequence was inspired by The Wizard of Oz, and was my first ever attempt at animation. The background image is a still from the 1939 movie.
The sequence is an attempt at a basic walk cycle and camera movement. The Tin Man was manually riggged using Maya's available joint tools - creating a joint network rooted at the spine. Foot and hand manipulators were used to help the animation process.

Software: Maya
